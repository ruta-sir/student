## CTE Expo Reflection

Going into the CTE Expo, I expected it to be a chance to quickly look at different electives and programs offered on campus. What I didn't expect was how much I would learn simply by talking to students and instructors about what they were pursuing and why they were passionate about it. The event ended up being less about the programs themselves and more about the people behind them.

---

### Every Path Has a Different Motivation

One of the most interesting parts of the expo was hearing the reasoning behind why students chose their electives. As I visited booths such as OCS, Robotics, DECA, Firefighter Services, Culinary Arts, and others, I realized that everyone had a different story.

Some students were drawn to programs because they aligned with a future career they wanted to pursue. Others joined because they discovered a personal passion, enjoyed the community, or wanted to challenge themselves with something new. The specific pathway mattered less than the motivation behind it.

That perspective helped me understand that there is no single "correct" path. Success isn't about following the same route as everyone else; it's about finding something meaningful enough to invest your time and energy into.

---

### Computer Science Connects More Fields Than I Realized

As someone interested in computer science, one of my biggest takeaways was seeing how technology intersects with nearly every elective at the expo.

The connection was obvious in places like Robotics, where programming and engineering work hand-in-hand. But I also noticed it in areas where I hadn't thought about it as much before. Business programs such as DECA rely heavily on technology, data analysis, and digital communication. Emergency services increasingly depend on software systems and modern technology. Even Culinary Arts incorporates technology through inventory management, ordering systems, and operational planning.

The more booths I visited, the more I saw computer science not as a standalone field but as a tool that strengthens countless other disciplines. It reinforced the idea that technological literacy is becoming valuable regardless of the career path someone chooses.

---

### Exposure Creates Opportunity

Another lesson I took away was the value of simply being exposed to new possibilities. Before attending the expo, there were several programs I knew very little about. Walking through the event allowed me to learn about opportunities I might never have explored on my own.

Even if I don't pursue many of these pathways directly, understanding them broadens my perspective. It helps me appreciate the different skills, challenges, and goals that exist across various fields. More importantly, it creates connections between disciplines that might otherwise seem unrelated.

Many of the most innovative ideas come from combining knowledge from different areas, and the expo highlighted just how much overlap exists between fields.

---

### What I'm Taking Forward

This experience reinforced several ideas for me:

- Different people pursue different paths for different reasons, and there is value in understanding those perspectives.
- Computer science has applications far beyond software development and can create impact across many disciplines.
- Exposure to new opportunities can broaden your understanding of both careers and personal interests.
- Passion and curiosity are often the driving forces behind meaningful learning and achievement.

The biggest takeaway from the CTE Expo wasn't learning about a specific elective. It was realizing how interconnected different fields really are. By exploring the booths and speaking with the people behind them, I gained a better understanding of the many directions students can pursue and how computer science can play a role in nearly all of them. The experience strengthened my appreciation for both the diversity of opportunities available and the importance of technology in shaping them.
